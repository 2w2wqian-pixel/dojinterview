📜 Contract Law 合約法
Valid Contract 有效合約 ├── Offer 要約 ── Clear intention (Gibson v Manchester CC) ├── Acceptance 承約 ── Unqualified assent (Hyde v Wrench) ├── Consideration 約因 ── Something of value (Chappell v Nestlé) ├── Intention 意圖 ── Legal relations (Balfour v Balfour) └── Certainty 確定性 ── Terms sufficiently certain (Scammell v Ouston)

English

  • Offer: Clear indication of willingness to contract on specific terms.
  • Acceptance: Unqualified assent — mirror image rule.
  • Consideration: Must be sufficient but need not be adequate. Past consideration invalid (exception: Pao On v Lau Yiu Long).
  • Intention: Commercial presumed; domestic not (rebuttable).
  • Certainty: Terms must not be vague.

中文

  • 要約：清晰表明願意按特定條款訂立合約。
  • 承約：無條件同意所有條款。
  • 約因：須足夠但不需充足。過去約因無效（例外：Pao On案）。
  • 意圖：商業協議推定有法律關係意圖。
  • 確定性：條款不能含糊。

Dangerous Drugs Ordinance Cap.134 — Trafficking: s.2 definition, s.4 offence. Also possession (s.8), manufacturing (s.6).

s.17 Obtaining Property by Deception: Obtains property belonging to another by deception (deliberate or reckless, by words or conduct); dishonestly + intention to permanently deprive.

Definition: No mens rea required for one or more elements. Examples: traffic offences, food/drug safety, environmental, regulatory. Defences: Due diligence / reasonable steps; reasonable mistake of fact (subject to statute).

MR towards X can be transferred to actual victim Y. Requirements: compatibility; no "double transfer" (A-G's Ref (No.3/1994)). Example: R v Latimer — D hit X with belt, missed and hit Y.

AR and MR must coincide in time. Exceptions: (1) Continuing Act (Fagan); (2) Duty (Miller); (3) Series of Acts / Single Transaction (Thabo Meli); (4) Dutch Courage (Gallagher).

🏛️ Administrative Law / Judicial Review 行政法／司法覆核
GCHQ Grounds ├── Illegality — Ultra vires, error of law, improper purpose, fettering, considerations ├── Irrationality/Wednesbury — So unreasonable no reasonable authority could come to it ├── Procedural Impropriety — Fair hearing, bias, natural justice └── Legitimate Expectation — Procedural & Substantive (Ng Siu Tung)

Proportionality in fundamental rights cases under Cap.383 HK Bill of Rights Ordinance.

Leave (O.53 r.4 RHC): Reasonably arguable (Po Fun Chan); Standing — sufficient interest (s.21K(3) HCO); Promptness — 3 months; No alternative remedy.

Amenability (Datafin): Statutory power presumed. Public function: but-for test, government underpinning, monopolistic power, public funding.

Ouster Clause: Not effective for void/ultra vires decisions (Anisminic).

  • Fettering: Discretion must be exercised with open mind (Venables; Li Tak Shing).
  • Considerations: Must consider mandatory relevant matters; must not consider irrelevant ones.
  • Error of Law: All errors reviewable (Anisminic; Page).
  • Error of Fact (E v Secretary of State): Mistake as to existing fact; established & verifiable; applicant not responsible; material.

Fair Hearing (Ridge v Baldwin): Right to be heard, notice, reasons (Doody; Oriental Daily), disclosure.

Bias (Deacons v White & Case): Actual, Presumed (pecuniary — Dimes), Apparent — "real possibility of bias" (Porter v Magill).

Sliding Scale (SPH v Town Planning Board) ├── Anxious Scrutiny → Fundamental rights ├── Heightened → Important interests ├── Standard Wednesbury → Ordinary └── Deferential → Policy/Economic

Substantive LE (Ng Siu Tung): Representation; clear & unambiguous; authority; knowledge. Departure only by overriding public interest. Procedural LE (Ng Yuen Shiu): Promise or regular practice.

O'Reilly v Mackman ├── Public law issues → Must use JR ├── Private law issues → Ordinary proceedings └── Exceptions → Collateral Attack (Wandsworth v Winder); Collateral Defence (Credit Suisse v Allerdale); Consent of Parties

🔍 Evidence Law 證據法
Evidence ── Testimony / Documentary / Real

Admissibility: Must be relevant. Excluded if prejudicial effect > probative value (Lam Tat Ming).

Competence: General (s.5 EO); D (s.54 CPO); spouse (s.57 CPO); child <14 (s.4 EO unsworn); MIP (s.3(b) EO court consent).

Exam-in-Chief: No leading questions; no bolstering. Hostile witness (s.12 EO). Cross-Exam: Leading allowed; duty to put case (Browne v Dunn). Rape Shield (s.154(3) CO).

Civil vs Criminal: Civil: written statements as evidence-in-chief; parties compellable. Criminal: oral exam-in-chief still common; accused has right to silence.

Affirmation: Solemn non‑religious declaration — same legal effect as oath. Oaths and Declarations Ordinance Cap.11. Used in court, affidavits, statutory declarations.

Definition: Out-of-court statement to prove truth of contents (Oei Hengky Wiryo). Exceptions: Business Records (s.22 EO); Computer (s.22A); Bank (s.20); Written Statements (s.65B CPO); Res Gestae; Dying Declaration (Nembhard).

Confession (Lam Tat Ming) ├── Voluntary — P prove BRD ├── Made to Person in Authority ├── No Inducement — (Chau Ching Kay 2-stage) └── No Oppression

Turnbull: Judge examine quality; warn jury; specific weaknesses; supporting evidence. Recognition can be mistaken (Gayle).

Good Character (Berrada): Credibility + propensity. Bad Character: Makin rule; Zabel Ali 4-step; shield s.54(1)(f) CPO.

Expert (Un Mark William): Relevant, necessary, reliable. Duties: independent (Ikarian Reefer). Voir Dire: Separate hearing; D's evidence not admissible for general issue (Ma Yee Keung). Stay (Ng Chun To): Fair trial impossible / abuse of process.

General rule: Only facts, not opinions. Exceptions (s.58 EO, civil only): Identity, age, physical/mental condition, value, speed, distance, weather — when opinion inseparable from facts.

Cross-examiner may ask without showing. If witness denies → may produce (s.13 EO). If admitted → affects credibility; if confirmed → contents become evidence (non-hearsay).

Out of Court: Allowed — may refer to notes shortly after event (Lee Wing Kan). No coaching (Wong Ka Ching). In Court: May request permission; contemporaneous record preferred; non-contemporaneous affects weight (See Wah Lun).

Abolished: Accomplices (s.60 CPO); Children (s.4A EO); Sexual offence complainants (s.4B EO). Residue: Real evidence still needs witness testimony (Wong Cho Shing); Perjury (s.43 CO); Judge may still give warning (Wong Chi King).

Witness Immunity: Absolute immunity from suit for evidence given in court. Cannot be sued for defamation. Advocate's Immunity: Abolished in UK; HK position: not yet abolished (pending reform).

Right to refuse if would expose self or spouse to proceedings. Limited to HK offences (Salt & Light). Does NOT apply to D's spouse in criminal proceedings (s.57(8) CPO). Statutory exceptions exist (e.g., s.63 Road Traffic Ordinance).

Montgomery v Lanarkshire [2015]: Doctor must disclose material risks that a reasonable patient would want to know. Bolam test no longer applies to consent.

✍️ Legal Drafting 法律草擬（中文作答）
中英文本地位 ├── 《釋義及通則條例》(Cap.1)第10B條 ── 中英文本均為真確文本，地位相同 ├── 《基本法》第九條 ── 中文和英文都是正式語文 ├── 律政司法律草擬科 ── 雙語文本法律權威完全相同 └── 歧義處理 ── 法院採用目的解釋，不預設某一語文優先

不存在「以英文為準」或「以中文為準」的主次關係，中英文本具同等法律效力。

舊式用詞（已減少）

  • "hereby"（茲）
  • "thereof/whereof"（關乎）
  • "hereinbefore/hereinafter"
  • "aforesaid"、"said"
  • "notwithstanding"（現用「儘管」）
  • "whereas"（鑑於）

現代草擬趨勢

  • 避免濫用"shall"→"must"/"is to be"
  • 避免"he"→"he or she"或"person"
  • 採用平易語言(Plain Language)
  • 去除冗餘，清晰易讀
  • 同等效力：中英文本皆為真確文本。
  • 目的解釋：歧義時尋求最能實現立法原意的解釋。
  • 一致性：同一概念使用一致的對應詞彙。
  • Plain Language：避免古雅詞彙。
  • 性別中立：避免性別定型代詞。
  • 結構清晰：條、款、項層級結構。

Q: "What is the role of the Secretary for Justice in upholding the rule of law?"

A: (1) Independent Prosecutions — based on evidence & public interest; (2) Legal Advice — advising Government on legality; (3) Legislative Drafting — ensuring Basic Law compliance; (4) Civil Litigation — representing Government; (5) Constitutional Guardian — protecting Basic Law consistency.
