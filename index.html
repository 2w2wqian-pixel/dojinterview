📜 Contract Law 合約法
Valid Contract 有效合約 ├── Offer 要約 ── Clear intention to be bound on specific terms (Gibson v Manchester CC) ├── Acceptance 承約 ── Unqualified assent; mirror image rule (Hyde v Wrench) ├── Consideration 約因/代價 ── Something of value; sufficient but not adequate (Chappell v Nestlé) ├── Intention to Create Legal Relations 意圖 ── Commercial presumed; domestic rebuttable (Balfour v Balfour) └── Certainty 確定性 ── Terms must not be vague or incomplete (Scammell v Ouston)

English

  • Offer: Must be certain, complete, and communicated.
  • Acceptance: Mirror image rule — any variation is a counter-offer.
  • Consideration: Must be sufficient but need not be adequate. Past consideration invalid (exception: Pao On v Lau Yiu Long).
  • Intention: Presumption — commercial agreements intend legal relations; domestic do not (rebuttable).
  • Certainty: Terms must not be vague or incomplete.

中文

  • 要約：清晰表明願意按特定條款訂立合約。必須確定、完整及傳達。
  • 承約：無條件同意所有條款。鏡像規則——任何變更即為反要約。
  • 約因：須足夠但不需充足。過去約因無效（例外：Pao On案）。
  • 意圖：商業協議推定有法律關係意圖；家庭協議則否（可推翻）。
  • 確定性：條款不能含糊或不完整。

Discharge 解除 ├── Performance 履行 ── Both parties fully perform ├── Agreement 協議 ── Mutual agreement to terminate ├── Breach 違約 ── Breach of condition → terminate + damages ├── Frustration 受挫 ── Supervening event makes performance impossible or radically different (Davis Contractors v Fareham UDC) └── Operation of Law 法律運作 ── Merger, bankruptcy, etc. Anticipatory Breach 預期違約 ── Party indicates in advance they will not perform (Hochster v De La Tour) Repudiatory Breach 廢棄性違約 ── Breach of condition → innocent party can terminate + damages
Anticipatory Breach: Innocent party may treat as breach and sue immediately OR wait for performance date.

⚔️ Criminal Law 刑法 🟢 Actus Reus | 🟡 Mens Rea
Homicide 殺人罪行 ├── Murder 謀殺 ── AR: unlawful killing + causation (substantial cause) | MR: intention to kill OR cause GBH (Woollin) ├── Unlawful Act Manslaughter ── AR: intentional act + criminally unlawful + dangerous (Church) | MR: MR of base offence only (Newbury) └── Gross Negligence Manslaughter ── AR: duty of care + breach + causation | MR: breach so bad = criminal negligence (Adomako)
Murder 謀殺
🟢 Actus Reus: Unlawful killing of a reasonable creature in being; causation — D's act must be substantial cause. Brain stem death = death. Accelerating death = killing.
🟡 Mens Rea: Malice aforethought — intention to kill OR cause GBH. Woollin oblique intent: virtual certainty test. Mercy killing = still murder (unlawful).
Unlawful Act Manslaughter (UAM) 非法行為誤殺
🟢 Actus Reus: (1) An intentional act; (2) The act is criminally unlawful; (3) The act is dangerous (objective test — Church: objectively foreseeable risk of some harm); (4) Causation.
🟡 Mens Rea: The MR of the base criminal offence only. No need to prove D foresaw harm (Newbury). Unlawful act must be intentional; need not be aimed at actual victim (A-G's Ref No.3/1994).
Gross Negligence Manslaughter (GNM) 嚴重疏忽誤殺
🟢 Actus Reus: (1) D owed V a duty of care; (2) D breached that duty; (3) D's breach caused V's death. Duty sources: contract, parent-child, assumption of responsibility (Stone v Dobinson), supervening fault (Miller).
🟡 Mens Rea (Culpability): Breach is so bad as to amount to criminal negligence. Adomako test: objective gross negligence. Or: subjective recklessness (Mak Wan Ling).
Non-Fatal Offences 非致命罪行 ├── Assault 襲擊 ── AR: Causing apprehension of immediate unlawful force | MR: Intention or Recklessness ├── Battery 毆打 ── AR: Infliction of unlawful force (direct/indirect) | MR: Intention or Recklessness ├── ABH (s.39 CO) ── AR: Assault/Battery + Actual Bodily Harm | MR: Same as assault/battery ├── GBH s.19 ── AR: Unlawfully cause GBH | MR: Maliciously (intention or recklessness) └── GBH w/ Intent s.17 ── AR: Unlawfully cause GBH | MR: Intention to cause GBH
Assault 襲擊
🟢 AR: Causing V to apprehend immediate unlawful force. Words can constitute assault (Ireland). Hostility NOT required (Shek Kwok Ngai).
🟡 MR: Intention or Recklessness as to causing V to apprehend immediate unlawful force.
Battery 毆打
🟢 AR: Actual infliction of unlawful force. Any touching, however slight, can be battery. Indirect infliction sufficient.
🟡 MR: Intention or Recklessness as to inflicting unlawful force.
ABH (s.39) 襲擊致造成身體傷害
🟢 AR: Assault or Battery + Actual Bodily Harm. Includes psychiatric injury (Chan-Fook).
🟡 MR: Same as assault/battery — no need to intend or foresee ABH.
GBH s.19 嚴重身體傷害
🟢 AR: Unlawfully cause grievous bodily harm (really serious harm).
🟡 MR: Maliciously — intention or recklessness as to causing some harm.
GBH w/ Intent s.17 意圖造成嚴重身體傷害
🟢 AR: Same as s.19.
🟡 MR: Intention to cause GBH. Recklessness insufficient. Specific intent offence.
Property Offences 財產罪行 ├── Theft 盜竊 (s.9) ── AR: Appropriation + Property + Belonging to another | MR: Dishonesty (Ghosh test) + Intention to permanently deprive └── Burglary 入屋犯法 (s.11) ── AR: (a) Entry as trespasser with intent; (b) Having entered, stealing/inflicting GBH | MR: Knowledge/recklessness as to trespass + ulterior intent
Theft 盜竊 (s.9)
🟢 AR: Appropriation (assumption of owner's rights — Gomez) + Property (including intangible) + Belonging to another (possession/control/ownership).
🟡 MR: Dishonesty (Ghosh test still followed in HK) + Intention to permanently deprive (s.7).
Burglary 入屋犯法 (s.11)
🟢 AR: (a) Entry as trespasser into building/part of building with intent to commit theft/GBH/damage/rape; OR (b) Having entered, stealing or inflicting GBH.
🟡 MR: Knowledge or recklessness as to being a trespasser + ulterior intent (for (a)). Key difference: burglary requires entry as trespasser into building.
Other Property Offences ├── Robbery 搶劫 (s.10) ── AR: Theft + force immediately before/at time | MR: MR of theft + intent to rob └── Criminal Damage 刑事毀壞 (s.60) ── AR: Without lawful excuse destroys/damages property | MR: Intention or recklessness (subjective — R v G)
Robbery (s.10)
🟢 AR: Theft + use or threat of force immediately before or at the time of theft. Appropriation as continuing act (Lockley).
🟡 MR: MR of theft + intent to rob. s.10(2): with intent to rob also guilty.
Criminal Damage (s.60)
🟢 AR: Without lawful excuse destroys/damages property belonging to another. Statutory defence s.64: (a) belief entitled to consent; (b) protect own property.
🟡 MR: Intention to destroy/damage OR recklessness (R v G subjective). Arson s.60(3).
Sexual Offences 性罪行 ├── Rape 強姦 (s.118 CO) ── AR: Sexual intercourse; V does not consent | MR: D knows or is reckless as to consent (Satnam; Morgan) └── Indecent Assault 非禮 (s.122 CO) ── AR: Assault + circumstances of indecency | MR: Intention to touch; indecent purpose if equivocal
Rape s.118
🟢 AR: Man has sexual intercourse with woman; V does not consent. Vitiating factors: force/threat, fraud, lack of capacity.
🟡 MR: D knows V does not consent OR is reckless. Satnam: "could not care less". Genuine mistaken belief even if unreasonable (Morgan).
Indecent Assault s.122
🟢 AR: Assault (battery) + circumstances of indecency (Choi Wai Lun). Under 16/MIP cannot consent.
🟡 MR: Intention to touch; indecent purpose if conduct equivocal. Honest mistaken belief in consent (Morgan).
Participation 參與責任 ├── Accessory (s.89 CPO) ── AR: Aid/Abet/Counsel/Procure | MR: Knowledge of essential facts + Intention to assist ├── Basic JCE ── Agreement to commit offence X → all liable for X └── Extended JCE ── Foresee additional offence Y as possible incident (Chan Wing Siu)
Accessory (s.89 CPO)
🟢 AR: Aid (help/assist); Abet (encourage at time); Counsel (encourage before); Procure (cause). Mere presence insufficient (Coney).
🟡 MR: Knowledge of essential facts + Intention to help/encourage principal.
JCE 共同犯罪企業
🟢 Basic: Agreement to commit X → all liable. Extended: If D2 foresees P may commit additional offence Y as possible incident → liable for Y.
🟡 MR: Must foresee MR of Y, not just the act (Chan Kam Shing).
Defences 抗辯 ├── Self-Defence ── Subjective belief + objective reasonableness (Palmer) ├── Duress ── Graham test; NOT for murder (Hasan) ├── Intoxication ── Voluntary: no defence for basic intent (Majewski) ├── Provocation ── s.4 Homicide Ord; sudden loss (Duffy) ├── Diminished Responsibility ── s.3 Homicide Ord; substantial impairment (Byrne) └── Insanity ── M'Naghten Rules; defect of reason; disease of mind
Self-Defence 自衛
🟢 Subjective: D genuinely believed force necessary (mistake can be unreasonable).
🟡 Objective: Force used was reasonable and proportionate (Palmer). If D started fight, may still rely if V's response disproportionate (Burns).
Duress 脅迫
🟢 Subjective: D impelled to act by fear of death/serious harm; threat directed at D/close family (Hasan).
🟡 Objective: Sober person of reasonable firmness would have acted same way (Graham). NOT available for murder.
Intoxication 醉酒
🟢 Voluntary: No defence for basic intent offences. May negate MR for specific intent offences.
🟡 Specific intent: murder, theft, robbery, GBH s.17, burglary. Basic intent: manslaughter, rape, assault, ABH, GBH s.19.
Provocation 激怒 (s.4 Homicide Ord)
🟢 Subjective: Actually provoked to lose self-control; loss must be sudden and temporary (Duffy); can be cumulative.
🟡 Objective: Reasonable person of same age/sex would have been provoked (Camplin).
Diminished Responsibility (s.3)
🟢 Abnormality of mind: State so different from ordinary (Byrne). Aetiology: arrested development, inherent causes, disease/injury.
🟡 Substantial impairment: Mental responsibility substantially impaired (Golds). Burden: D on balance.
Insanity (M'Naghten Rules)
🟢 Defect of reason: Actual impairment of reasoning. Disease of mind: internal cause (Kemp; Sullivan).
🟡 Result: Did not know nature/quality of act OR that it was wrong. Burden: D on balance. Verdict: not guilty by reason of insanity.
Automatism 自動症
🟢 Total destruction of voluntary control (Bratty). External cause → full acquittal. Internal cause → insanity only.
🟡 Burden: D raises evidence; P must prove BRD conduct not involuntary.
Dangerous Drugs Ordinance (Cap. 134) — Trafficking: s.2 definition, s.4 offence. Possession s.8; Manufacturing s.6.
Deception & Handling ├── s.17 Obtaining Property by Deception ── AR: Obtains property by deception (words/conduct) | MR: Dishonestly + intention to permanently deprive ├── s.18 Pecuniary Advantage ── AR: Similar to s.17 but for pecuniary advantage | MR: Dishonestly └── s.24 Handling Stolen Goods ── AR: Arranging to receive / assisting retention | MR: Knowledge or belief goods stolen
🟢 s.17: Obtaining property belonging to another by deception. s.18: pecuniary advantage. s.24: handling stolen goods.
🟡 s.17/18: Dishonestly. s.24: knowledge or belief goods stolen.
Inchoate Offences 不完整罪行 ├── Attempt (s.159G CO) ── AR: Act more than merely preparatory | MR: Intention to commit full offence (Gullefer) ├── Conspiracy (s.159A CO) ── AR: Agreement on course of conduct involving offence | MR: Intention to agree + intention/knowledge of facts (Saik) └── Incitement (CL) ── AR: Encouraging/pressurising another; must be communicated | MR: Intention to incite (Cox)
Attempt s.159G
🟢 AR: Act more than merely preparatory (question of fact). Gullefer: jumping onto track = merely preparatory.
🟡 MR: Intention to commit full offence. Conditional intent suffices. Khan: recklessness as to circumstance may suffice.
Conspiracy s.159A
🟢 AR: Agreement to carry out course of conduct involving offence.
🟡 MR: Intention to agree + intention/knowledge of relevant facts (Saik).
Incitement (CL)
🟢 AR: Encouraging/pressurising; must be communicated (Cox). Can incite "the world".
🟡 MR: Intention to incite + belief incitee would commit offence.
Other Key Concepts ├── Strict Liability ── No MR required for some elements; defences: due diligence ├── Transferred Malice ── MR towards X transfers to Y; no double transfer (Latimer; A-G Ref No.3/1994) └── Coincidence Principle ── AR and MR must coincide; exceptions: continuing act, duty, series of acts, Dutch courage (Fagan; Miller; Thabo Meli; Gallagher)
Strict Liability
🟢 No MR required. Examples: traffic, food safety, environmental. Defences: due diligence, reasonable mistake of fact.
Transferred Malice
🟢 MR towards X can transfer to Y. Requirements: compatibility. No double transfer.
Coincidence
🟢 AR and MR must coincide in time. Exceptions: continuing act (Fagan), duty (Miller), series of acts (Thabo Meli), Dutch courage (Gallagher).
🏛️ Administrative Law / Judicial Review 行政法
GCHQ Grounds ├── Illegality 不合法 ── Ultra vires, error of law, improper purpose, fettering, considerations ├── Irrationality/Wednesbury ── So unreasonable no reasonable authority could come to it ├── Procedural Impropriety ── Fair hearing, bias, natural justice └── Legitimate Expectation ── Procedural & Substantive (Ng Siu Tung)
Proportionality for human rights cases under Cap.383 HK Bill of Rights Ordinance.
Leave: Reasonably arguable, standing (sufficient interest), promptness (3 months), no alternative remedy.
Amenability (Datafin): Statutory power presumed; public function test.
Ouster Clause: Ineffective for void decisions (Anisminic).
Fettering: Open mind required (Venables). Considerations: Mandatory relevant vs irrelevant. Error of law: All reviewable. Error of fact (E v Secretary of State): 4 requirements.
Fair hearing: Right to be heard, reasons (Doody). Bias: Apparent bias — real possibility (Porter v Magill).
Sliding scale: Anxious scrutiny → deferential. Substantive LE (Ng Siu Tung): Clear representation, departure only by overriding public interest.
O'Reilly v Mackman: Public law must use JR. Remedies: certiorari, prohibition, mandamus, declaration, injunction, damages — all discretionary.

🔍 Evidence Law 證據法
Evidence 證據 ── Testimony · Documentary · Real └── Admissibility: Relevant; excluded if prejudicial > probative (Lam Tat Ming)
Competence: General, D, spouse, child (unsworn), MIP. Exam-in-chief: no leading; hostile witness. Cross-exam: leading allowed, duty to put case (Browne v Dunn).
Civil vs Criminal: Civil uses witness statements; criminal often oral; accused right to silence.
Solemn non‑religious declaration; same effect as oath.
Definition: Out-of-court statement to prove truth. Exceptions: business records (s.22 EO), res gestae, dying declaration, etc.
Requirements: Voluntary, person in authority, no inducement (Chau Ching Kay), no oppression. Mixed statement admissible.
Turnbull: examine quality; warn jury. Good character (Berrada). Bad character: Makin rule, shield s.54(1)(f).
Expert: relevant, necessary, reliable. Voir dire: separate hearing. Stay: fair trial impossible.
Prosecution: legal burden BRD. Defences: evidential burden on D; insanity/diminished resp. legal burden on D (balance).
